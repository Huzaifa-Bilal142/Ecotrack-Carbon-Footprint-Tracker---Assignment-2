Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about carbon footprints and EcoTrack

A carbon footprint is the total amount of greenhouse gases (including carbon dioxide and methane) that are generated by our actions. The average carbon footprint for a person in the United States is 16 tons, one of the highest rates in the world. Globally, the average is closer to 4 tons.

Our calculator uses standard emission factors from reputable environmental agencies to provide estimates of your carbon footprint. While it may not capture every single aspect of your lifestyle, it gives a good approximation of your major sources of emissions. For the most accurate results, try to provide as much detailed information as possible.

For most people in developed countries, the major contributors are:

  • Transportation (especially air travel and personal vehicles)
  • Home energy use (electricity, heating, cooling)
  • Food choices (particularly meat and dairy consumption)
  • Consumer goods and services

Some of the most effective ways to reduce your carbon footprint include:

  • Reducing air travel or choosing direct flights
  • Using public transportation, biking, or walking when possible
  • Switching to renewable energy sources for your home
  • Eating less meat and dairy, especially beef and lamb
  • Reducing energy consumption at home (LED bulbs, efficient appliances)
  • Buying fewer new products and choosing sustainable options

Visit our Tips page for more detailed recommendations.

Climate change is one of the most pressing issues facing our planet, and human activities are the primary driver. By understanding and reducing your carbon footprint, you can:

  • Help mitigate climate change and its devastating effects
  • Save money through more efficient energy use
  • Improve air quality and public health
  • Support the transition to a sustainable economy
  • Inspire others to take action through your example

We recommend calculating your footprint:

  • When you first start tracking (to establish a baseline)
  • After making significant lifestyle changes (moving, changing diet, etc.)
  • Every 3-6 months to track your progress

Regular tracking helps you see the impact of changes you've made and identify new areas for improvement.
